1 % stereo_gui
2 % Stereo Camera Calibration Toolbox (two cameras, internal and external calibration):
3 %
4 % It is assumed that the two cameras (left and right) have been calibrated with the pattern at the same 3D locations, and the same points
5 % on the pattern (select the same grid points). Therefore, in particular, the same number of images were used to calibrate both cameras.
6 % The two calibration result files must have been saved under two seperate data files (Calib_Results_left.mat and Calib_Results_right.mat)
7 % prior to running this toolbox. To generate the two files, run the classic Camera Calibration toolbox calib.m.
8 %
9 % INPUT: Calib_Results_left.mat, Calib_Results_right.mat -> Generated by the standard calibration toolbox on the two cameras individually
10 % OUTPUT: Calib_Results_stereo.mat -> The saved result after global stereo calibration (after running stereo calibration, and hitting Save stereo calib results)
11 %
12 % Main result variables stored in Calib_Results_stereo.mat:
13 % om, R, T: relative rotation and translation of the right camera wrt the left camera
14 % fc_left, cc_left, kc_left, alpha_c_left, KK_left: New intrinsic parameters of the left camera
15 % fc_right, cc_right, kc_right, alpha_c_right, KK_right: New intrinsic parameters of the right camera
16 %
17 % Both sets of intrinsic parameters are equivalent to the classical {fc,cc,kc,alpha_c,KK} described online at:
18 % http://www.vision.caltech.edu/bouguetj/calib_doc/parameters.html
19 %
20 % Note: If you do not want to recompute the intinsic parameters, through stereo calibration you may want to set
21 % recompute_intrinsic_right and recompute_intrinsic_left to zero, prior to running stereo calibration. Default: 1
22 %
23 % Definition of the extrinsic parameters: R and om are related through the rodrigues formula (R=rodrigues(om)).
24 % Consider a point P of coordinates XL and XR in the left and right camera reference frames respectively.
25 % XL and XR are related to each other through the following rigid motion transformation:
26 % XR = R * XL + T
27 % R and T (or equivalently om and T) fully describe the relative displacement of the two cameras.
28 %
29 %
30 % If the Warning message "Disabling view kk - Reason: the left and right images are found inconsistent" is encountered during stereo calibration,
31 % that probably means that for the kkth pair of images, the left and right images are found to have captured the calibration pattern at two
32 % different locations in space. That means that the two views are not consistent, and therefore cannot be used for stereo calibration.
33 % When capturing your images, make sure that you do not move the calibration pattern between capturing the left and the right images.
34 % The pattwern can (and should) be moved in space only between two sets of (left,right) images.
35 % Another reason for inconsistency is that you selected a different set of points on the pattern when running the separate calibrations
36 % (leading to the two files Calib_Results_left.mat and Calib_Results_left.mat). Make sure that the same points are selected in the
37 % two separate calibration. In other words, the points need to correspond.
